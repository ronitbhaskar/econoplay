Case Study: Bertrand Competition – Price Wars in Telecom

Competitive Pricing Overview

The Bertrand competition model describes how firms compete by setting prices rather than quantities. In telecom markets, companies often engage in aggressive price competition to gain market share, leading to dynamic and sometimes unstable outcomes.

Market Dynamics

Firms simultaneously choose prices for homogeneous products. The firm with the lower price captures the entire market, forcing rivals to lower their prices. This interaction often drives prices down to marginal cost in equilibrium.

Insights & Implications

Bertrand competition results in fierce price competition, sometimes resulting in zero economic profits. However, product differentiation, capacity constraints, or repeated interaction can soften this outcome.

Real-World Application

Telecom firms frequently use strategic pricing, promotions, and bundling to retain customers and avoid pure Bertrand outcomes. Regulatory policies also impact pricing strategies.
