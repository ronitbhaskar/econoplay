Case Study: Cournot Duopoly – Boeing vs Airbus

Strategic Competition Overview

In this case study, we examine Boeing and Airbus, two dominant players in the global aircraft manufacturing industry. Both firms decide how many units (airplanes) to produce, knowing that their output influences market price. This interaction forms the basis of the Cournot model in oligopoly economics.

Market Assumptions

The market demand is linear, and marginal costs are constant. Each firm decides simultaneously on quantity, and the price is determined by total quantity in the market. Competitive pressures, strategic interdependence, and reaction functions are at the core of this analysis.

Deeper Analysis

The Cournot model is a fundamental concept used to model duopolies where firms choose output quantities rather than prices. By using reaction functions, each firm determines its best output level in response to the other's decision. The Nash equilibrium in this model gives us a point where neither firm has anything to gain by changing only its own output.

Real-World Insight

Historically, both firms have used production adjustments and pricing strategies to edge each other out in different markets, such as Asia and the EU. The Cournot model is an abstraction of these strategic decisions. Government regulations, subsidies, and technological advancements further influence their competitive strategies.
