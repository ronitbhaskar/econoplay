Bertrand Competition – Price Wars in Telecom

Analyzing strategic price setting in fiercely competitive telecom markets using Bertrand logic and 3D profit surfaces.

Profit Analysis

The Bertrand model predicts that in equilibrium, each firm sets its price equal to marginal cost (p = c). When prices differ, the lower-priced firm captures the entire market. We simulate this through dynamic profit mappings.

πₐ = (pₐ - c) × Q × e^( -|pₐ - p_b| / 10 )

The profit for Firm A decreases rapidly when prices rise above its competitor. This exponential decay captures competitive elasticity.

Strategic Sensitivity

We now visualize the payoff surface from a reaction function standpoint. Firms are highly sensitive to small deviations in pricing when in symmetric positions.

Sensitivity = 10 × e^( -|pₐ - p_b| / 5 )

The steeper the fall, the less stable the equilibrium. Identifying these cliffs in the payoff structure allows for strategic anticipation.
